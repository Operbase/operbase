# Operbase — Usage Guide

This guide explains how to use Operbase step by step. It is written in plain English.

---

## The quickest way to log anything

Every page has a **"Log:"** bar at the top with three buttons: **I bought**, **I made**, **I sold**.

Tap any of them. A dialog opens with plain-English prompts. When you save, the result screen shows you how much money came in (or how much you kept as profit). After a few sales, it will start remembering your best result and tell you when you beat it.

You do not need to go to the Stock, Production, or Sales pages for everyday logging — this bar is the fastest path.

---

## Before you start (first time setup)

Operbase works best when you follow this order the first time:

1. **Add your stock** — the ingredients you buy
2. **Record a production run** — what you made
3. **Log a sale** — when money comes in
4. **Check the dashboard** — see if you made money

You do not have to do all of this right away. Even logging sales with no stock data gives you a record of your money in.

---

## Step 1: Add stock

Go to **Stock** in the menu.

**What to add:** Everything you buy to make your products — flour, sugar, butter, packaging boxes, bags, etc.

**How to add it:**
- Tap a preset (Flour, Sugar, Butter…) if it matches what you use, OR
- Tap "Other item" and type the name yourself.

**What the form asks:**
- **Name**: What do you call it? (e.g. All-purpose flour)
- **How much did you buy?** How many bags/kg/packs you have right now.
- **What did you pay?** The price for one bag/pack/kg.

**Advanced (optional):** If you buy in bags but measure in cups, tap "Buy in bags but measure in cups?" to set both units and how many cups are in one bag.

**Why this matters:** When you record production, Operbase deducts stock and calculates what that run cost you.

---

## Step 2: Record a production run

Go to **Production** in the menu.

**When to do this:** Every time you finish making a batch of something.

**How to record it:**
1. Tap **Record production**.
2. Pick the product from the presets (Bread, Croissants…) or type any name.
3. Enter how many you made.
4. If some are NOT for sale (samples, gifts), enter that number.
5. Tap **Save production**.

**Optional — track what you used:**
Tap "Track what you used (optional)" to add ingredient lines. This shows you the cost of the run and helps calculate profit on sales.

**What the page shows:**
- Made / Sold / Left / Lost totals across all runs.
- Each run in a table — tap the arrow to see the full breakdown.

---

## Step 3: Log a sale

Go to **Sales** in the menu.

**When to do this:** Every time you sell something and money comes in.

**How to log it:**
1. Tap **Log sale**.
2. Search for the product or pick a production run.
3. Enter how many you sold.
4. Enter the price per item.
5. Tap **Save sale**.

**From a run vs quick sale:**
- **From a production run** — links the sale to the exact run you recorded. Gives the most accurate cost and profit numbers.
- **Quick sale** — works without a run. Uses the average cost from recent runs of that product.

**The preview shows:**
- Cost (what it cost you to make what you sold)
- Money in (what the customer paid)
- You keep (the difference — your profit)

---

## Step 4: Check the dashboard

Go to **Home** in the menu (or the Operbase icon).

**What you see:**

- **Big headline** — whether you made or lost money today, and how much.
- **Items waiting to sell** — how many finished items you have left, and what ingredient cost is at stake.
- **Today in numbers** — items made, items sold, money in, and what you kept.
- **Running low** — any ingredients below their alert threshold.

---

## How to check your pantry

Go to **Stock** → tap the **This week** tab.

This shows every ingredient that moved this week:
- How much you had at the start of the week
- How much you used in production
- How much you bought (added)
- How much you have right now

---

## How to see more details on a sale

Go to **Sales** → click the arrow on any sale in the table → see cost, money in, and profit for that sale.

Tap **See daily breakdown** to see revenue per day.

---

## How to use the "What happened today?" log

The **Log bar** at the top of every page (and the three cards on the home page) is the fastest way to record your day.

### I bought

Tap **I bought**. Enter:
- What you bought (type freely or pick from your existing stock)
- How many you got
- How much you paid (optional)

After saving, the screen shows how much you now have in stock and a nudge toward your next production run.

### I made

Tap **I made**. Two steps:

**Step 1**: Enter the product name and how many you made. Tap **Next →**.

**Step 2**: "Did you sell any right away?"
- Tap **💰 Yes, I sold some** → enter how many and the price each → Save.
- Tap **⏰ No, not yet** → saves the production run immediately.

The result screen shows profit (if you sold) or motivates you to sell while fresh.

### I sold

Tap **I sold**. Enter:
- What you sold
- How many
- Price per item

The result screen shows how much you kept as profit. After a few sales, it will tell you when you beat your best sale.

---

## How to log a giveaway or waste

If you have finished items that will not be sold:

1. Go to **Production**.
2. Find the run in the table and tap the arrow to expand it.
3. Tap **Gave away / lost**.
4. Enter how many and choose what happened (gave them away or did not sell).

This keeps your "items left" number accurate.

---

## How to use the assistant

At the bottom of the **Home** page, there is an "Ask your business" box.

You can ask things like:
- "What stock do I have left?"
- "What did I use this week?"
- "How much did I make today?"
- "How many items did I sell today?"

The assistant reads your live data and answers in plain English. It does not use the internet — everything comes from your own records.

---

## Tips for good numbers

- **Use the same product name** when you record production and when you log a sale. This links them for accurate cost calculation.
- **Add ingredient costs** when you restock. The more cost data you add, the more accurate your profit numbers.
- **Log production before sales** when possible. Linking a sale to a run gives exact cost; quick sales use an average.
- **Record giveaways and samples** so your "items left" number stays accurate.
